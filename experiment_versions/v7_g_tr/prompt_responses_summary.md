## Key Observations

- **Prime asymmetry (up/down > left/right):** Reported independently by subjects 036, 039, and 043 — worth checking against the response-time data.
- **Flanker effect awareness:** Subjects 036 and 042 explicitly noticed the manipulation; 036's description (refocusing triggered a switch) is the clearest qualitative support for the cascade mechanism.
- **Fatigue and perceptual noise:** Subjects 037, 042, and 043 all describe breakdown of coherent percepts under prolonged viewing — spontaneous direction loss, 4-corner illusion, or "vision errors."
- **9-pair condition difficulty:** Subjects 036 and 040 specifically flag that large arrays were harder, with more desynchronised motion across pairs.
